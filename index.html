Where and how do I start a change?

As an change agent,

you should get some time for upfront planning and design a strategy to introduce your new idea, and create your own concrete action plan to reach your goal.

Good news ...

there is many successful strategies already tested and compiled to reuse. You don't have to start all over from scratch.


Patterns to Introduce New Ideas

Example

Once upon a time, I have joined a big company where everything was new to me. I was hired to start a agile transformation.

Test The Water


When a new opportunity presents itself, see if is any interest by using some of the patterns in this language and evaluating the result.

Pick Your Battles


Before you expend your energy in conflict, ask yourself whether you believe the issue is really important and if you have the resources to carry your fight through to the end.

The Right Time


Consider the timing you schedule events or when you ask others for help.

Be aware of those times when people are likely to be the busiest. Schedule events and requests for help outside those times.

Ask For Help


Since the task of introducing a new idea into an organization is a big job, look for people and resources to help your efforts.

My Concrete Action Plan was:


  1. Test The Waters
  2. Pick Your Battles
  3. The Right Time
  4. Ask For Help
